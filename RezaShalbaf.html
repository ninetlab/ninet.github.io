Meet Reza.

Reza Shalbaf received the M.Sc. degrees in Biomedical Engineering from the Amirkabir University of Technology, in 2006 and the PhD degree in Bioelectrical Engineering from Iran University of Science & Technology, in 2013. From 2011 to 2012, he worked as short term researcher responsible for mathematical modeling and EEG correlates of the transition between conscious states in the laboratory of D. A. Steyn-Ross and J.W Sleigh at University of Waikato, New Zealand. After graduating, Reza had the opportunity to work in Iranâ€™s principal medical device company named Saadat designing both hardware and software of depth of anesthesia monitor device using EEG signal in operation room. Looking for a change of scenery, Reza moved out to British Columbia and joined NINET lab in 2016 as a Postdoc Researcher Fellow. Reza currently focuses on EEG biomarker as predictor of clinical response to rTMS in treatment resistant depression, studies dynamic functional connectivity and its relationships with brain disorder, and as well as conducts neurophysiology aspects of the lab.
